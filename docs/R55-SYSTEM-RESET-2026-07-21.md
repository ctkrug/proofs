## Outcome

The research loop was rebuilt around evidence events, typed route value, compact campaign state,
bounded deterministic experiments, and fail-closed artifact receipts. A monitored production pass
then tested the new incumbent: complete known-class boundary blocking for a fixed order-30 core.

The mathematical pilot passed its scoped gate. Two independent embedding enumerators agreed exactly
for supplied source records 21 and 12 and for a complement negative control. A third checker replayed

and rejected both a one-edge corruption and a mapping corruption. The pilot projects the complete

This is verified infrastructure and a justified next discriminator, not a Ramsey result. No known
isomorphism class was fully blocked, no new order-42 graph was found, and the maintained bound remains
`43 <= R(5,5) <= 46`.

## What changed end to end

- Every model-backed R55 review now requires a concrete unconsumed evidence event; the timer merely
  polls six times daily. Completed lab work emits events automatically.
- Route selection uses explicit gate proximity, contribution value, decisiveness, novelty confidence
  and risk, scope, reuse value, model cost, and CPU cost. Missing fields receive visible conservative
  defaults. Repetition and terminal states reduce or eliminate eligibility.
- State reconciliation closes stale, orphaned, and terminal-route leads and rejects empty/error
  strategies. The seeded incumbent is `r55knownclassembeddingblock2026`.
- The prompt receives one bounded canonical route brief instead of overlapping full state, dossier,
  roadmap, and prior-art dumps. Two specialist reviews run in parallel and return bounded memos.
- Work expected to exceed two minutes is routed to the checkpointed deterministic lab. System changes,
  global installs, and writes outside the problem workspace are prohibited; the production Codex
  sandbox is `workspace-write`.
- Every attempt snapshots the workspace and attempts to emit an immutable delta manifest and validation
  receipt. Computed progress without explicit immutable evidence is downgraded. Mutable projections
  cannot support a claim.
- Prior art now includes generalized gluing, SRG(45) constraints, the 656-extension DRAT computation,
  SAT Modulo Symmetries, and formal/certificate precedents. Field-progress gates are explicit.
- Reusable rules for future open-problem campaigns live in
  `docs/RESEARCH-DESIGN-GUIDELINES.md`.

### Evidence-receipt finding

The model incorrectly included `CHECKPOINT.md` and `records/research-state.json` in `evidence_files`.
Both are mutable projections. The new evidence layer rejected the claim, downgraded the engine result
to `no_progress`, and did not project it into canonical engine research state. This is a successful
fail-closed safety test, but it means the engine-level receipt is invalid even though the committed
problem-repository artifacts and independent replay are intact.

The prompt contract was immediately tightened and regression-tested so future attempts explicitly
exclude mutable projections from evidence claims. The invalid attempt remains immutable; it was not
silently repaired or promoted.
